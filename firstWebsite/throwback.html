Women who shaped the history of computer science

The history of computer science and coding is filled with ambitous women, who had seen the great potential of computing and dedicated hard-work and their expertise for the development of this exciting field. Here are their inspiring stories!

Grace Hopper (1906 - 1992) - The Queen of software

Grace hopper was a pioneer in computer programming. Among her many achievements, she worked on UNIVAC I which was the first commercial computer in the US and invented FLOW MATIC, the first English-like data processing language. Furthermore, she is credited for coming up with the term 'computer bug'.

"Humans are allergic to change, they love to say, 'We've always done it this way.' I try to fight that. That's why I have a clock on my wall that runs counter-clockwise."

Sister Mary Kenneth Keller (1913 - 1958) - First female computer science PhD

Sister Mary Kenneth Keller was the first women to recieve a PhD in computer science in the US in 1965. She assited in the development of the BASIC computer language and her work and contributions made computer study and STEM subjects more accesible and available for women.

"having an information explosion... and information is of no use unless it's available"

Katherine Coleman Goble Johnson (1918 -) - Stargazer

Katherine Coleman Goble Johnson was a US mathematician who made significant contributions to the US aeronautics and space programs. If you want to get to know more about her life watch the movie 'Hidden figures'(2016) which is based on her life.

Henriette Avram (1919 - 2006) - The woman who revolutionized libraries

Henriette Avram was o a computer programmer and systems analyst who developed the MARC format, the international data standard for bibliographic and holdings information in libraries. This invention had a revolutionizing effect on the practice of librarianship, making possible the automation of many library functions and the sharing of bibliographic information electronically between libraries using pre-existing cataloging standards

Beatrice Helen Worsely (1921 - 1972) - Canada's female computer pioneer

Worsely was the first female computer scientist in Canada. Throughout her career she published around 17 papers in professional journals and numerous articles.

Annie Easly (1933 - 2011) - The rocket scientist

Annie Easly was an African-American computer scientist, mathematician, and rocket scientist. She was a leading member of the team which developed software for the Centaur rocket stage and one of the first African-Americans in her field. Easly worked for the Lewis Research Centre of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration.

Radia Perlman (1951 -) - The mother of the Internet

Radia Perlman is primarily recognised as the creator of the algorithm behind the Spanning Tree Protocol, which is a crucial part of internets underlying foundation.

"In engineering, the point is to get the job done, and people are happy to help. You should be generous with credit, and you should be happy to help others."

Carol Shaw (1955 -) - The videogame designer

Carol Shaw was the first female videogame designer. Shaw programmed the classic scrolling shooter, River Raid. She is also responsible for 3-D Tic Tac Toe, Super Breakout, and Happy Trails.
